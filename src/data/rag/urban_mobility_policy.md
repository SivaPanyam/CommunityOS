# Smart City Standard Operating Procedure: Urban Mobility & Traffic Management

**SOP Document Reference: SOP-TRAF-2026-V1.2**
**Authorizing Department: Department of Transportation & Urban Mobility Division**

## SECTION 1: SIGNAL CONTROLLER OPTIMIZATION (SOP SECTION 2.1)
1. **Dynamic Congestion Response**:
   - If a corridor (e.g. **Downtown Expressway** or **5th Ave & Pine St**) reaches a Congestion Index > 0.80, the master signal timing plans must be modified.
   - Adjust the active cycle length to 120 seconds, allocating an extra 15% green phase length to the primary bottleneck direction.
   - For public bus routes, enable active transit signal priority (TSP) at all intersections.

## SECTION 2: HAZARD REPAIRS & ROADWAY INTEGRITY (SOP SECTION 2.2)
1. **Potholes & Sidewalk Blocks**:
   - Citizen complaints categorized as **Critical Priority Potholes** (e.g., severe tire damage potential) must be filled within 24 hours of report validation.
   - Standard cold-mix asphalt can be used for temporary patches under cold conditions, but permanent hot-mix must be laid when ambient temperature exceeds 15°C.
   - Sidewalk blocks obstructing wheelchair access (ADA compliance) must be cleared immediately by Waste Management or Forestry divisions.
