# Smart City Standard Operating Procedure: Emergency & Disaster Response

**SOP Document Reference: SOP-EMG-2026-V3**
**Authorizing Department: Municipal Safety Council & Emergency Management Agency**

## SECTION 1: FLOOD EMERGENCY MANAGEMENT (SOP SECTION 1.1)
1. **Trigger Thresholds**: 
   - **Flood Risk Level Medium**: Active monitoring begins when rainfall exceeds 10mm/hour or reservoir level exceeds 85%.
   - **Flood Risk Level High**: Immediate alert and deployment when water accumulation on highways exceeds 5cm, or reservoir level is >90%.
2. **Action Plan**:
   - First responders must pre-deploy water pumps to critical underpasses, specifically **Metro Bridge Underpass** and lower **Westside Tunnel**.
   - Dispatch Traffic Division to initiate signal detours on flooded arterial routes.
   - Set up safety shelters at **City Community Gymnasium** and **Northside Elementary School**.

## SECTION 2: HAZARDOUS ACCIDENTS & ROADWAY BLOCKAGES (SOP SECTION 1.2)
1. **Trigger Thresholds**:
   - Collision involving more than 2 vehicles, or blocking major highway expressways (e.g. **Downtown Expressway**).
2. **Action Plan**:
   - Immediate dispatch of 2 Ambulance units and 1 Police Highway Patrol unit.
   - Re-route incoming traffic at previous exits to avoid 3km blockages.
   - Update digital smart-road billboards within 5km radius with warning message: "COLLISION AHEAD - MERGE LEFT - EXPECT DELAYS".

## SECTION 3: INDUSTRIAL OR ELECTRICAL FIRE RESPONSES (SOP SECTION 1.3)
1. **Trigger Thresholds**:
   - Transformer explosion, grid short circuit, or structure fires in **Industrial Sector Block 12**.
2. **Action Plan**:
   - Dispatch a minimum of 3 Fire Trucks and establish a 1km safety isolation perimeter.
   - Request grid operator (**GRID-B**) to isolate electrical circuits to prevent domino failures.
   - Direct ambient air monitoring stations to assess toxic smoke release.
